Do’s and Don’ts for ATS-Friendly Resumes

Do’s

  • ✓ Incorporate Relevant Keywords: Use keywords directly from the job description to align your resume with the role's requirements.
  • ✓ Tailor Your Resume for Each Job: Customize your resume to highlight experiences and skills that are most relevant to the specific job you're applying for.
  • ✓ Use a Simple and Consistent Format: Stick to professional fonts (e.g., Arial, Calibri) and avoid excessive formatting.
  • ✓ Save Your Resume in an ATS-Friendly Format: Use file types like Word (.docx) or PDF that are compatible with ATS.
  • ✓ Focus on a Professional Summary: Include a concise summary at the top that highlights your key qualifications and career objectives.
  • ✓ Highlight Achievements with Metrics: Quantify accomplishments using numbers and percentages to demonstrate impact. Example: “Increased team efficiency by 20% through process optimization.”
  • ✓ Use Standard Section Headings: Stick to recognizable headings such as “Work Experience,” “Education,” and “Skills.”
  • ✓ List Relevant Certifications and Skills: Highlight certifications, technical skills, and tools relevant to the job.
  • ✓ Use Action-Oriented Language: Start bullet points with strong action verbs like “Led,” “Managed,” “Developed,” or “Implemented.”
  • ✓ Test Your Resume for ATS Compatibility: Use online ATS checkers to ensure your resume can be successfully parsed.

Don’ts

  • ✗ Avoid Fancy Fonts, Graphics, or Colors: ATS systems may fail to parse resumes with creative designs or decorative elements.
  • ✗ Don’t Use Columns, Tables, or Charts: Stick to a single-column format to ensure proper readability by ATS.
  • ✗ Avoid Abbreviations Without Explanation: Provide the full form of terms with abbreviations in parentheses. Example: “Search Engine Optimization (SEO).”
  • ✗ Don’t Include Irrelevant Information: Avoid personal details like age, marital status, religion, or hobbies unless relevant to the role.
  • ✗ Avoid Overloading with Keywords: Use keywords naturally and in context; do not overstuff the resume.
  • ✗ Don’t Use Headers or Footers for Important Information: ATS systems may skip content in headers and footers, so keep critical information in the main body.
  • ✗ Avoid Long Paragraphs: Use bullet points for readability instead of lengthy blocks of text.
  • ✗ Don’t Submit a Resume with Errors: Spelling and grammar mistakes can harm your credibility. Proofread thoroughly.
  • ✗ Don’t Copy Job Descriptions Directly: Rewrite content in your own words while including relevant keywords.
  • ✗ Avoid Excessive Length: Limit your resume to one or two pages, focusing on relevant experiences and skills.
